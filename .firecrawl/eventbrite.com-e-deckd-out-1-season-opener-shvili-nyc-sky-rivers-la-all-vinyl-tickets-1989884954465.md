**About Shvili:** A music lover and selector from Tbilisi, Georgia. In the field of Music Therapy, classically trained pianist, with deep underground experience.Hailing from the vibrant city of Tbilisi, Georgia, Shvili has been immersed in the world of music from an early age. Growing up as a classical pianist, she honed her skills and earned several accolades showcasing her dedication to her craft. Her musical journey was enriched by singing in both chamber choirs and church choirs, further broadening her artistic horizons.

Upon moving to the United States, Tatia attended an esteemed art school with a focus on music, where she continued to expand her musical repertoire. It was during these formative years that she discovered her profound connection with dance music. The pulsating beats of the underground scene resonated deeply within her, setting the stage for her future endeavors.This experience solidified her desire to make a name for herself in the bustling metropolis of NYC.

Emerging as a pandemic artist, she utilized the time of societal isolation to perfect her art. With unwavering determination, she delved into the world of vinyl, crafting her unique sound and style. Her efforts have paid off, as she has had the honor of sharing the stage with respected artists such as Francesco del Garda, Anthea, Liquid Earth, Tini, Velasco, Nicola Cruz, Andrey Pushkarev, and many more.

Known for her timeless records and playful spirit, Shvili infuses her sets with infectious grooves that captivate audiences. Her performances have graced some of the most prestigious clubs and events in the United States, including Public Records, ReSolute, Un\_Mute, Flash DC, ATV Records, Apollo Studios, Jolene Sound Room, and more.

With a rich musical background and a passion for creating unforgettable experiences, Tatia is poised to share her vibrant energy and distinctive sound with the world. Her journey is a testament to the power of music to transcend boundaries and connect souls on the dance floor.

**About Sky Rivers:** Sky Rivers began his journey in 1995, drawn into the pulse of acid house and the storytelling power of the dancefloor. Raised in Los Angeles, he immersed himself in DJ culture before becoming part of San Francisco’s scene from 2001–2006 under his first alias "Boris Collage"

Following a formative tour through South America and the loss of his parents in 2008 and 2009, Rivers stepped away from the spotlight, redirecting his focus toward filmmaking and visual storytelling. Throughout this period, he remained deeply connected to music—collecting, curating, and playing intimate sets within close circles.

In late 2023, an invitation from Monica Escalante to play In Transit in Los Angeles marked his return. The set reignited a renewed sense of purpose, opening a new chapter that bridges his history in dance music with years of refined artistic perspective.
